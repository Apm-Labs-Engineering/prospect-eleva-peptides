## What's Actually Happening in Your Body After 30

Your body runs on signals. Not just hormones (though those matter) — peptides. Short chains of amino acids that act as precision messengers between your cells, your organs, and your brain. They tell your muscles to repair. They tell your metabolism how to process energy. They regulate inflammation, sleep quality, cognitive performance, and cellular longevity.

Here's what most people don't know: peptide production starts declining in your late 20s and early 30s. It's gradual — you don't notice it all at once. But over time, those signals get quieter. And when the signals fade, the symptoms follow.

That 2pm crash? A signal problem. The recovery that used to take 24 hours now taking 72? A signal problem. The weight that won't move no matter how clean you eat or how consistently you train? Often, a signal problem.

This isn't a character flaw. It isn't laziness. It's biology.
